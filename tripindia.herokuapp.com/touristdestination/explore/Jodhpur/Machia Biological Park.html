Machia Biological Park

Jodhpur , Rajasthan

Description

Introduction :The vision of the Machia Biological Park is to create an opportunity for the citizens of Jodhpur to have a biological park very close to the city in Machia Forest Block of 604 ha. near Kayalana lake. Jodhpur is having a tradition of very good in-situ conservation. The vision is to create one of the best example of ex-situ conservation and at the same time the best tourist spot

Hsitory

Theorized in 1983, the recreation center fills in as a satellite zoo for the Jodhpur Zoo, built up by Maharaja Umaid Singh in 1935. The administration of the zoo has been depended with the forest administration of the Rajasthan Government since 1956.

How To Reach Machia Biological Park

The closest bus stop is Aakhaliya Chouraha Bus Stop, which is 4 and a half kilometres from the park. Taxis and auto-rickshaws may be used to arrive at this destination. For a more scenic route, one can take Chopasni road, which runs across the length of Kaylana Lake to the venue. This is a longer journey though.

Entry Fee

Indians: INR 30, Students: INR 15, Foreign Nationals: INR 300, Children below the age of 5: No Entry Fee

Timings

Summer (March to October): 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM, Winter (October to March): 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM Closed every Tuesday

Wildlife

This park is home to desert foxes, common foxes, jungle cats, desert cats, monitor lizards, porcupines, spiny-tailed lizards, hedgehogs, crocodiles, gavials, spotted deer, black bucks, gazelles, royal Bengal tigers, Asiatic lions, leopards, tortoises, rabbits, and mongooses. Further plans are being made to add jackals, hyenas, and sloth bears.
